The Hollywood Sign

History on The Hollywood Sign

The Hollywood Sign was originally erected in 1923 as an advertisement for a real estate development called "Hollywoodland. Each letter stood 30 feet wide and 50 feet tall, and the sign was lit up by 4,000 bulbs. As the development faded, the sign fell into disrepair. In 1949, the city took control, removing the "LAND" and leaving the iconic "HOLLYWOOD" part. Over the years, it has undergone several renovations, including a major one in 1978 where the sign was replaced with steel letters. Today, the Hollywood Sign is a symbol of the entertainment industry and a famous landmark in LA.

Interesting Facts

Size

The sign's letters are 45 feet (13.7 meters) tall. Each letter is 30 feet (9 meters) wide.

Construction

The sign was built in 1923 by the Hollywoodland real estate company as an advertisement. It originally read "HOLLYWOODLAND" and was made of wood and steel. It was illuminated with 4,000 light bulbs.

Cultural Significance

The sign has become a symbol of the film industry and Hollywood itself. It represents the global entertainment capital and is often used as a backdrop in films, TV shows, and photographs. It’s also a major tourist attraction in Los Angeles, with views of the sign from Griffith Park.

Visting Hours

The Hollywood Sign itself doesn’t have specific visiting hours since it’s located on a hilltop in Griffith Park, which is a public space.
